You run programs on Linux. You know how to use a command line, and check top. But there's something missing! Sometimes a coworker will show you a cool trick and you're like "WHAT?! YOU CAN DO THAT? WHAT ELSE DON'T I KNOW?".

Understanding the fundamentals of how your operating system works can really level you up as a programmer -- it makes it dramatically easier to debug your programs and write efficient software. But who has time to read a 1500 page tome about operating systems? Not me! In this book, I'll teach you all the stuff I wish I knew about Linux 5 years ago, and tell you stories about how learning basic operating systems concepts has helped me solve real programming problems.
